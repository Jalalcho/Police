Leadership

The leadership of the Russian police consists of various high-ranking officers who oversee national and regional police operations. They play a crucial role in maintaining law and order.

Key leadership positions include:

  • The General of the Police – Head of all police operations in Russia.
  • The Colonel-General of the Police – Assist in specific police divisions such as criminal investigations, public security, etc.
  • The Lieutenant-General of the Police – Responsible for law enforcement in specific regions of Russia.
  • The Major-General of the Police – Responsible for law enforcement in specific regions of Russia.

Ranks of the Russian Police

Rank Description
General of the Police (Генерал полиции) The highest rank, held by the head of the entire police force.
Colonel General (Генерал-полковник) One of the highest ranks, overseeing national-level police operations.
Lieutenant General (Генерал-лейтенант) Oversees large-scale operations or entire sectors of the police.
Major General (Генерал-майор) The first general officer rank, commanding regional or specialized forces.
Colonel (Полковник полиции) Holds strategic command over large regional forces or specialized branches.
Lieutenant Colonel (Подполковник полиции) Commands major police stations or regional offices.
Major (Майор полиции) A higher rank with command over significant police units.
Captain (Капитан полиции) Commands police departments or acts as second-in-command in larger stations.
Senior Lieutenant (Старший лейтенант полиции) Typically holds more administrative or specialized duties within police units.
Lieutenant (Лейтенант полиции) In charge of larger squads or specific departments.
Junior Lieutenant (Младший лейтенант полиции) The starting rank for commissioned officers, usually given to academy graduates.
Senior Warrant Officer (Старший прапорщик полиции) Higher responsibility than a warrant officer, often overseeing complex operations.
Warrant Officer (Прапорщик полиции) A technical expert or senior supervisor within the police force.
Sergeant Major (Старшина полиции) The highest non-commissioned officer rank, acting as a senior supervisor for sergeants and other lower ranks.
Senior Sergeant (Старший сержант полиции) Senior among sergeants, commanding a larger group or providing specialized support.
Sergeant (Сержант полиции) Often the first rank with significant supervisory duties.
Junior Sergeant (Младший сержант полиции) A non-commissioned officer responsible for supervising small teams.
Private (Рядовой полиции) The entry-level rank for police personnel, equivalent to a private in the military.

Definition of the Russian Police

The Russian police (Полиция России) is the federal law enforcement agency of Russia. It operates under the Ministry of Internal Affairs and is responsible for maintaining public order, investigating crimes, and enforcing laws.
